About me

I am a third year Mechanical Engineering student at Illinois institute of Technology in Chicago, IL. I am very passionate about engineering and science. For as long as I can remember I have had a very curious and experimental nature. Creating, breaking down, and understanding things has always been my hobby. I believe being an engineer is not just a career, it means being creative, being a volunteer, and providing sustainable solutions to daily problems.

Working at the Idea Shop prototyping lab at IIT has helped me explore my passion for engineering. As student staff, my job is to provide consultancy to students, staff, and faculty on a project, idea or a design. I walk them through the design process of prototyping and help them create economic solutions which they can implement quickly and efficiently. Through my work experience for two years I have learned how to work on and repair several machines like 3-D Printers, Laser Cutters, and Milling machines. I also am proficient at using different CAD softwares like Solidworks, AUTOCAD, AUTODESK INVENTOR, and Rhinoceros.

Besides working at the Idea Shop, I had the opportunity to perform research under Prof. Mahesh Krishnamurthy on autonomous robotic solutions. The goal of my research was to create a algorithm that could be easily be implemented on a robotic vehicle to make it fully autonomous with the use couple of range finding sensors. I designed a prototype robotic vehicle to test these algorithms, and the robot was capable of obstacle detection and avoidance, and could also navigate to any given nav-point.

In addition to academics, I am also involved in extracurricular activities, that expand my knowledge in science and engineering. I am the Lead Mechanical Engineer of NASA Robotic Mining Club(RMC) at IIT. Despite Being a first year team, we stood third over all in the mining competition. Over forty universities around the world took part in the competition, and the goal of the competition is to design a autonomous robot capable of mining in extraterrestrial environments. NASA simulates a mining environment similar to one on mars where teams earn design points based on the efficiency of the robot, the weight of the robot, the data consumed by the robot, and even how weather-proof is the robot!
